I just have to share this recipe.  All my kids love it and it is quick and easy.  Great way to use up leftover rice too.  You can of course alter the ingredients to suit your family.

Fried Rice

Recipe Type: Main
Cuisine: Asian influence
Author: Jacinta Gleeson Munn
Lovely way to use up leftover rice. We use some lovely local made cabana in our recipe, but of course this can be substituted for 2 more rashers of bacon if need be. To make it vegetarian, simply add some tasty flavoured tofu instead.
Ingredients
  • 4-5 cups cooked, cold rice
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 3 lettuce leaves, finely sliced
  • 2 spring onions (green onions), finely chopped
  • 1/2 brown onion, finely chopped
  • 2 rashers bacon
  • 10cm piece cabana, chopped finely
  • salt, to taste, approximately 1 tspn (I use Celtic salt)
  • approximately 1 tspn tamari/soy sauce to add to the eggs
  • Oil for cooking
Instructions
  1. Whisk eggs with some tamari/soy sauce.
  2. Add a little oil to the wok, then pour in egg mix. Cook through. Put aside.
  3. Place wok back on burner and add cabana and bacon. Cook until brown. Put aside.
  4. Place onion, spring onions, and if needed, a little oil in the wok. Cook until onion is transparent.
  5. Add in cold rice and stir until rice is hot.
  6. Add in cabana, bacon, grated carrot and salt to taste. Mix well.
  7. Once combined, add in lettuce.
  8. Turn off heat.
  9. Serve up into individual bowls or as part of a shared meal.
